Apple Pie for M16

A mini art showcase comprising of three poems, three AI generated images, and one piano song. It was created as part of a McBride class I took where we corresponded virtually with another class in Ukraine and conducted a cultural exchange. The class was discussed in this NPR article.


It's called "Apple Pie for M16" because while the Ukrainians were telling us of a particularly delicious apple pie they eat in Ukraine, to which the Americans were extremely interested to try, one of the Ukrainian students jokingly said something along the lines of: "let's make a deal, you can send us some F16s, and we'll send you apple pie". I initially misheard him, thinking he said "M16s", but the title still works!


Works were inspired by Ukrainian courage, Petrykivka art style, and spiritual transmutation.

"Chicken of Kiev" - Prompt: Petrykivka phoenix rising from the ashes

Burn

My stomach turns inside out
Shivers skim over my skin
I can't draw breath—
Am I in the face of death or someone I adore?

Like holding a sleeping grenade
A tenderness too powerful to sustain
It can turn one bird into a fleet
Or any heart into a blade

Whether it’s love or dying I taste,
The tremor feels the same
If no pain means nothing to save
I'd rather burn in the flame.

Revenge For a Friend

I have a friend who died today
Kind soul, turned warrior
Future husband, father, sculptor
Sucked into a destructive dream's game

Each one of us gone
Not just a body
But an unborn vision
A story left unwritten

I sit alone, safe from the slaughter
With the dreams I've given up
The painting I've put off
The number I never called
I've lost the scale of what matters.

A war born from men before us,
Their ambitions pressed into our bones.
And as a hollow blooms in my chest, I wonder
What could we build,
If we refused to let go?

His spirit remains in me
I open my lungs and breathe it in
It tells me to carry our dreams on, to cling to them
That's how we'll take our revenge

"Fireworks" - Prompt: Petrykivka fireworks bursting

The Mirage

I am the little man
Surrounded by tanks
And words that can only blame

Saying 'fuck you' in spite of the odds
That's when I feel free
I am the ruler of my own state

I have something eternal that power can't take
It tries to rake out of me what it desperately craves
A sense of self not dictated by rank

With each bullet hole, a leak in its own soul
To be covered up with billboards, lies, and paper mache
Until all that's left is a mirage, an empty gaze

So I look at the starving bear on the horizon
And grin as it fades
I have no reason to be afraid
